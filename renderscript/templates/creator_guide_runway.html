References are your continuity anchor

Drift is expected. Strong references will not eliminate drift, but they reduce expensive reroll cycles.

  • Start lean: cast, location look, style frame, and hero props.
  • Keep naming exact so reference loading stays predictable shot to shot.
  • Avoid overlays and heavy processing in source images.
  • Picture-first rule: no burned text, subtitle overlays, or watermarks in source refs.

Page 4 of 5

Cut picture first, then finish sound in post

In AI production and traditional post alike: lock rough cut rhythm first, then ADR/VO, SFX, and final mix.

Picture-first policy

  • No burned-in text, subtitles, or captions in generated images.
  • If text appears in frame, reroll cleanly instead of repairing downstream.
  • Build ADR/VO, SFX, ambience, and mix after rough cut timing is stable.

Keepers turn taste into a repeatable system

Judgment still leads. The Keeper Sheet structures that judgment so iteration stays sharp and efficient.

  • Mark one keeper per shot and log the reason it won.
  • If continuity drifts, adjust refs/prompt scope and reroll only the failing beat.
  • Protect what already works, then finish toward a confident export.
