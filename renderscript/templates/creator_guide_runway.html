Build references with intent

Reference quality drives keeper quality. Drift is expected, but clean assets reduce expensive reroll cycles.

  • Start lean: style, location, cast, and hero props.
  • Keep naming exact so reference loading stays predictable shot-to-shot.
  • Avoid overlays and noisy processing in source images.

Rough cut first, then post sound

In practical filmmaking terms: picture decisions first, then ADR/VO, SFX design, and final mix.

Picture-first policy

  • No burned-in text, subtitles, or captions in generated images.
  • If text appears, reroll that shot cleanly.
  • Keep audio craft in post once the rough cut timing is stable.

Keepers turn taste into repeatable decisions

You still need judgment. The Keeper Sheet gives that judgment structure so iteration stays efficient.

  • Mark the strongest keeper for each shot and note what made it work.
  • If continuity drifts, adjust references or scope and reroll only what failed.
  • Protect good material, then finish toward a confident export.
