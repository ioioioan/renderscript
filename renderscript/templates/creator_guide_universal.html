References are where continuity starts

Drift is normal in AI generation. Clean references do not remove it completely, but they reduce rework across the whole cut.

  • Quick way to start: make a minimal set first, then expand only where the story needs it.
  • What works best: stable naming, neutral lighting, and no text overlays in source images.
  • Keep every reference where the package expects it, so shot generation stays friction-free.

Page 4 of 5

Picture first, audio after rough cut

Industry workflow still applies in the AI era: lock picture intent first, then finish ADR/VO, SFX, and the mix in post.

Picture-first policy

  • No burned-in text, subtitles, or captions in generated shots.
  • If text appears in frame, reroll that take instead of cleaning it later.
  • Build ADR/VO, SFX, ambience, and music once the rough cut rhythm feels right.

Page 5 of 5

Use keepers to guide iteration, not guesswork

RenderPackage will not make outputs deterministic, but it gives you a repeatable system for reducing unnecessary rerolls.

  • Use the Keeper Sheet to mark one keeper per shot and track why it works.
  • If continuity slips, reroll only the failing beat instead of rebuilding the whole scene.
  • Keep what is already strong, then polish toward final export.
